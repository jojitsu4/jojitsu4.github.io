Chronic Pain Management A Guide For Clinicians

General Physical Exam

Common Pain Sites/How to Focus the Exam

Abdominal Pain: Evaluate for abdominal wall (myofascial) pain using Carnett’s test.

Arm (upper): Neck, Shoulder, Rotator cuff

Buttocks: Hip internal rotation, SI joint (FABER’s test, palpate SIJ, thigh thrust, Gaenslen’s test, pelvic compression and distraction)

Groin: Hip internal rotation, SI joint (FABER’s test, palpate SIJ, thigh thrust, Gaenslen’s test, pelvic compression and distraction), pubic ramus

Knee: 1. Observe alignment and for effusion, 2. AROM vs. PROM (hip and knee), 3. Palpation (joint line, tendons, Pes anserine bursa), 4. Provocative testing:
  • McMurray (meniscus)
  • Apley/Lachman (instability)
  • Patellar compression (Patellofemoral Syndrome)
  • Noble (IT band)

Low Back: Hip internal rotation, knee exam, SI joint (FABER’s test, palpate SIJ, thigh thrust, Gaenslen’s test, pelvic compression and distraction), leg length discrepancy, spinal alignment (scoliosis, kyphosis); quadratus lumborum/ erector spinae (iliocostalis lumborum)/gluteus medius myofascial pain

Neck/Upper Back: Range of motion of neck, shoulder

Sciatica: Piriformis exam (piriformis syndrome)

Shoulder: Active ROM (Apley’s scratch test) vs. Passive ROM
  1. Rotator cuff muscle strength testing with resistance:
    • Supraspinatus: Empty can
    • Infraspinatus: External rotation
    • Subscapularis: Belly press/liftoff
    • Teres minor: Hornblower’s
  2. Impingement: Neer sign and Hawkins-Kennedy test
  3. Biceps: Speed’s and Yergason’s
  4. Acromioclavicular: Cross arm test

Thigh: Hip internal rotation, IT band (lateral pain), Ober’s test, direct palpation, SI joint (FABER’s test, palpate SIJ, thigh thrust, Gaenslen’s test, pelvic compression and distraction), lumbar spine (dermatomes)
