Stepped Care Medications for Neuropathic Pain

Trigeminal neuralgia

Carbamazepine: Start at 50 mg qweek; titrate 50 mg qweek to 100 mg bid. Max 1,200 mg/d. In patients who are Chinese, Thai, or Malaysian, consider testing for HLA-B* 1502 allele.â€¨If present, increased risk of toxic epidermal necrolysis and Stevens-Johnson syndrome

Gabapentin: Use if CBZ not tolerated

Baclofen: Add if needed. Do not use in older adults

Peripheral Neuropathy/Postherpetic Neuralgia (PHN)

2017 ADA Guidelines recommend starting with pregabalin or duloxetine for diabetic peripheral neuropathy, and avoiding opioids

Lidocaine patch: 5% 1 - 3patches; 12h on / 12h off. Efficacy for PHN only

Gabapentin: For younger pts with normal CrCl. Start at 300mg daily, uptitrate by 300mg daily or every other day to pain relief (max 3600 mg/d). Renal dosing: CrCl 30-59: max 600mg BID; CrCl 15-20: max 300mg BID; CrCl < 15: 300mg daily. Supplement post dialysis
Example: Day 1 300mg, Day 2: 300mg BID, Day3: 300mg TID, Day 4 300/300/600, etc
Note: In elderly, starting dose 100mg qHs and titrate by 100mg weekly

Pregabalin: 25-50 mg qhs; increase 25-50 mg q7d to 100 mg bid; max 300 mg qd. Renal dosing: CrCl 30-60: 150-300 mg/d; CrCl 15-30: 75-150 mg/d; CrCl < 15: 25-50 mg/d. Supplement dose after dialysis

Venlafaxine: 37.5 mg/d; Increase by 37.5 mg qweek to150 mg/d. Max 225 mg/d

Duloxetine: 20-30 mg/d; increase to 60 mg/d in 7d. Not recommended in ESRD or CrCl < 30

Nortriptyline or desipramine: 10 mg qhs; increase 10 mg q7d to max of 50 mg qhs. Watch for QT prolongation; EKG prior to starting in older adults

Opioids: If necessary. Add if some effect with other drugs; substitute if no effect with other drugs.
